Section 01 · Executive Briefing

A phased framework for sovereign capability.

This initiative presents a phased National Security Modernization, Strategic Resource Development, and Sovereign Capability Program designed to strengthen Ecuador's national security capacity, modernize intelligence and surveillance capabilities, protect strategic resources, and establish a sustainable sovereign defense and industrial ecosystem.

Through strategic stakeholder engagement, capital deployment coordination, critical infrastructure investment support, and economic development initiatives, the program is structured to deliver both immediate capability enhancements and a long-term pathway toward sovereign resilience, industrial growth, and national security modernization.

Strategic End State

An Ecuador capable of independently securing, sustaining, producing, integrating, and protecting the systems, infrastructure, intelligence architecture, and strategic resources required to support national security and economic development objectives.

  • Sovereign ISR & intelligence fusion capability
  • National targeting architecture
  • Validated critical minerals & rare earth assessments (conditional development track)
  • Domestic manufacturing & sovereign defense production
  • Strategic infrastructure protection
  • Long-term institutional modernization

Strategic Rationale

Ecuador faces a convergence of national security challenges — transnational criminal organizations, narcotics trafficking, illegal mining, border vulnerabilities, maritime corridors, and emerging strategic resource opportunities.

Traditional fragmented approaches address individual symptoms but fail to create the institutional architecture necessary for long-term sovereign capability development. This initiative establishes a phased modernization framework purpose-built for that architecture.

Strategic Resource Security & Critical Minerals Development

Workstream III — Contract Structure Clarification

Workstream III is available under three engagement options:

  • 90-Day — Introductory Assessment
  • 6-Month — Foundation Program
  • 12-Month — Advisory Information Edition

Selection of the 12-Month Strategic Partnership Program for Workstream III does not automatically authorize or initiate strategic resource development, mining activities, extraction projects, processing facilities, commercialization efforts, or downstream infrastructure development.

Instead, the 12-Month engagement allows and is intended for future advisory support, assessment refinement, strategic resource validation, stakeholder engagement, and development of strategic recommendations.

The implementation of a strategic roadmap with regards to commercialization, operations, stakeholder engagement, and execution activities shall be revisited and formally negotiated upon successful completion of Stage 2 (6 Months), based upon validated mapping outcomes, geological findings, infrastructure feasibility, commercial viability, and Government of Ecuador priorities.
